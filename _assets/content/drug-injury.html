What Makes a Drug Defective?

Although approvals by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) make medications appear safe, this isn’t always true. Defects can be discovered months or years after a drug is initially approved, causing severe complications to the people who take them. Drug injuries can result from a variety of factors, including:
  • Defective or negligent manufacturing processes
  • Inadequate warning labels
  • Harmful interactions with other drugs
